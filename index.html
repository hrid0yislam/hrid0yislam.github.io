About Me

My journey in electrical engineering began with a fascination for how power systems could be transformed to serve humanity more sustainably. This curiosity evolved into a dedicated pursuit of smart grid technologies and renewable energy integration, culminating in my Master's degree from UiT Arctic University of Norway, where I specialized in smart distribution networks and voltage control systems.

Through my research, I've come to understand that the future of energy systems lies not just in technological advancement, but in the intelligent coordination of diverse energy sources. My Master's thesis on coordinated voltage control in smart distribution networks with high PV penetration using the IEEE 37-bus test system represents more than academic achievementâ€”it embodies my commitment to solving real-world challenges in renewable energy integration.

Currently, as a Research Assistant on the BRIDGE project, I'm exploring the frontiers of microbial fuel cell power management systems, combining biological processes with advanced control strategies. This interdisciplinary approach reflects my belief that innovation emerges at the intersection of different fields. My work involves developing real-time data acquisition systems and optimizing energy harvesting efficiency, contributing to the broader vision of sustainable energy solutions.

What drives me is the profound impact that electrical engineering can have on global sustainability. From my early work on piezoelectric energy harvesting systems to my current research in smart grids, I've consistently sought to bridge theory and practice, always with the goal of creating systems that are not only technically sound but also environmentally responsible and economically viable.
